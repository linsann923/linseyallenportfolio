01 | Piggies

Piggies is a personal finance tracking and budgeting service.This project is a personal collaboration with software engineer Ryan Jensen. This project is still in progress

Context

My brother Ryan is a skilled software engineer who is the founder of Piggies, a personal finance tracker and budgeting system. Piggies has been an ongoing project of Ryan's over the past year. However, while his coding skills have built the website, the user flow and features are not as well developed as he wants them to be. I, on the other hand, was very interested in collaborating as a UX designer and software engineer. So, we decided to team up to revamp the UX and UI design of Piggies to increase its legitimacy.

Approach

First, we started with Brand Guidelines. Ryan knew in his mind what Piggies was supposed to feel and look like, but we were able to sit down and define the brand in a very clear format. Here is our latest version!

User Flow

After establishing the brand identity, I dissected the current website for key functions and the smartest layout. I decided to zone in on three key flows.

Flows and Wireframes

The first step was to create the onboarding process for the first flow. I took time to explore industry design standards for onboarding processes. I also deducted what components to include in the onboarding process. In my mind, the right use of the onboarding process was to establish all the components the user needed to use Piggies website and features. This included linking accounts (deposits, credit lines, investments, loans, other, etc), starting the budget, and adding any saving goals (piggies). What's Next

I'll be focused on building the other flows' wireframes and testing them. I'll update soon!
